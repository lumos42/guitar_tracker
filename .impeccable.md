## Design Context

### Users
Casual hobbyist guitarist — plays for the love of it, 20-30 min a few times a week. Wants something encouraging and simple, not analytical. They pick up the app the same way they pick up the guitar: because they want to play, not because they want to fill out a spreadsheet.

### Brand Personality
Warm. Focused. Inviting.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark — guitarists practice in bedrooms, late at night, under warm lamp light
- **References**: Spotify / Apple Music — album art as hero, music-native, familiar patterns
- **Anti-references**: Dashboards, grids of stats, clinical data views, anything that feels like a productivity tool
- **First emotion**: "Let's play" — energetic and inviting. The app should make them want to pick up the guitar.

### Design Principles
1. **Album art is the hero** — let cover art drive visual interest; the app frames it, not competes with it
2. **One action at a time** — each screen has a clear primary action; no decision paralysis
3. **Minimal chrome, maximum content** — UI disappears behind the music
4. **Warmth over precision** — slightly golden, slightly soft; not cold and clinical
5. **Encouraging by default** — empty states celebrate the start of a journey, not an empty list
